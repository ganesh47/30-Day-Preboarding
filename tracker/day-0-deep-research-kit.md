# Day 0 Deep-Research Kit (Issue 000)

This kit converts **Issue 000 — Day 0 Baseline and Operating Rules** into an execution-ready study pack with curated reading, docs, videos, LinkedIn Learning paths, and quizzes.

## 1) What “good” looks like for Day 0
By end of Day 0, you should have:
1. A one-page baseline brief (strengths, weak spots, assumptions).
2. 30-day success metrics with numeric targets.
3. Personal operating rules for cadence and artifact quality.
4. A risk-question backlog (10+ questions).
5. A completed quiz scorecard (to benchmark Day 0 confidence).

---

## 2) Curated docs and references (high signal first)

### A. Credit risk principles and governance
1. **BIS: Principles for the Management of Credit Risk**  
   https://www.bis.org/publ/bcbs54.htm  
   Why: Canonical foundation for underwriting standards, portfolio management, and controls.

2. **Basel Committee: Principles for the Sound Management of Operational Risk**  
   https://www.bis.org/publ/bcbs195.htm  
   Why: Day 0 operating rules should include execution/ops risk, not only underwriting risk.

3. **OCC Handbooks index (commercial credit-related supervision guidance)**  
   https://www.occ.treas.gov/publications-and-resources/publications/comptrollers-handbook/index-comptrollers-handbook.html  
   Why: Practical examiner lens on policies, underwriting quality, and controls.

### B. Credit reporting and information quality
4. **World Bank: General Principles for Credit Reporting**  
   https://documents1.worldbank.org/curated/en/662161468147557554/pdf/701930WP0Box370rinciples2011final.pdf  
   Why: Helps structure assumptions on data completeness, data quality, and model input risk.

5. **IFC SME Finance resources**  
   https://www.ifc.org/en/what-we-do/sector-expertise/financial-institutions/sme-finance  
   Why: Useful for understanding practical constraints in lending systems and MSME underwriting.

### C. Credit cycle / macro context (to stress your assumptions)
6. **IMF Global Financial Stability Reports**  
   https://www.imf.org/en/Publications/GFSR  
   Why: Forces Day 0 assumptions to be macro-aware (rates, refinancing risk, default cycle pressure).

7. **Federal Reserve Financial Stability Report**  
   https://www.federalreserve.gov/publications/financial-stability-report.htm  
   Why: Framework for writing risk-backlog questions tied to systemic vulnerabilities.

8. **FRED (St. Louis Fed) — macro data portal**  
   https://fred.stlouisfed.org/  
   Why: Use for baseline charts (policy rate, unemployment, delinquency proxies) in your brief appendices.

## 5) Day 0 quiz pack (self-assessment)

Score each question 0/1. Record confidence (Low/Med/High). Target: **>= 8/12**.

### Quiz A — Lending judgment fundamentals (6)
1. A company shows strong EBITDA growth but rising receivable days and weak operating cash flow. What risk does this signal first?
2. Why is repayment source quality more important than collateral quality at origination?
3. Name two assumptions that can make DSCR look safer than reality.
4. Which is a stronger early warning signal: one covenant breach or 3 months of collection slippage without breach? Why?
5. What is one way portfolio concentration can hide behind “good individual deal memos”?
6. Define one situation where declining a loan creates more long-term franchise value than approving it.

### Quiz B — Operating rules and execution (6)
7. What is the minimum set of sections your daily artifact should include to be decision-useful?
8. Which metric best captures learning quality: hours studied, pages read, or decision accuracy trend? Why?
9. Give one lead indicator and one lag indicator for credit process quality.
10. What “red-team” question should be asked before every approval recommendation?
11. How would you detect if your month is becoming “busy but not improving judgment” by Day 7?
12. What evidence would make you revise your baseline assumptions immediately?

## 7) 90-minute Day 0 execution plan
- **0–15 min:** Write baseline snapshot + assumptions.
- **15–40 min:** Read BIS credit risk principles (skim + annotate).
- **40–55 min:** Read one macro stability source (Fed or IMF) and note 3 implications.
- **55–70 min:** Watch one short video (BIS/Fed/IMF channel).
- **70–85 min:** Complete quiz pack and score confidence.
- **85–90 min:** Write reflection and next-day adjustment.

If you want, the next step can be a filled-out **Day 0 Brief draft** based on your current profile so you can edit instead of starting from blank.
