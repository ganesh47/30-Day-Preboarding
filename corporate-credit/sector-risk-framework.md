# Sector Risk Framework

## Practical lens
Sector risk determines baseline volatility before borrower-specific effects.

## Cyclicality
- Demand elasticity, pricing power, and capacity overhang
- Sensitivity to macro variables (rates, commodities, FX)

## Regulation
- Licensing constraints, compliance costs, policy discontinuity risk
- Exposure to government counterparties or policy-linked demand

## Demand drivers
- Concentration by customer type and contract structure
- Substitution risk and technology disruption pathways

## Questions I would ask a credit team
1. What is the sector's historical drawdown pattern in bad cycles?
2. Which single external variable explains most defaults in this sector?
3. Are we relying on temporary tailwinds as if they are structural?
4. How does sector stress change collateral liquidation values?
