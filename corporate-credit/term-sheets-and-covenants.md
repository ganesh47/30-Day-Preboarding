# Term Sheets and Covenants

## Practical lens
Term sheets are risk-allocation documents. They are not legal boilerplate.

## Loan structure
- Instrument type, tenure, amortization profile
- Pricing (base + spread + reset logic)
- Conditions precedent and drawdown controls

## Covenants (financial and operational)
- Financial: DSCR, leverage, minimum net worth, liquidity thresholds
- Operational: reporting timelines, capex limits, related-party restrictions
- Covenant design should trigger action before value erosion is irreversible.

## Security package
- Pledge: ownership interest with enforcement pathways
- Mortgage: immovable asset collateral and enforceability timeline
- Charge: claims over current/fixed assets with ranking clarity

## Repayment structures
- Bullet, amortizing, step-up, cash sweep-linked models
- Match repayment profile with cash generation pattern

## Questions I would ask a credit team
1. Which covenant breach would most likely precede default?
2. Is collateral enforceable within a practical recovery window?
3. Does repayment design assume optimistic cash conversion?
4. What are the top two negotiated protections we refused, and why?
