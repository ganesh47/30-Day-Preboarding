# Financial Statement Analysis

## Practical lens
The purpose is to convert reported numbers into a repayment view.

## Income statement, balance sheet, cash flow linkage
- Revenue quality and margin stability indicate operating resilience.
- Balance sheet reveals leverage, liquidity buffers, and working-capital intensity.
- Cash flow statement validates whether accounting earnings convert into cash.

## EBITDA vs cash flow
- EBITDA is a starting signal, not repayment cash.
- Adjust for working capital absorption, maintenance capex, taxes, and debt service.
- Ask: "What cash is left after keeping the business alive?"

## Working capital cycle
- Analyze receivable days, inventory days, payable days.
- Stress scenarios: delayed collections, inventory obsolescence, supplier tightening.
- Identify hidden leverage embedded in working capital needs.

## DSCR, leverage, coverage
- DSCR: debt service capacity under base and downside cases.
- Net leverage: debt burden relative to sustainable earnings.
- Interest and fixed charge coverage: resilience to rate and margin shocks.

## Questions I would ask a credit team
1. Which line item in cash flow is most likely to break first under stress?
2. Are we underwriting one-time EBITDA or repeatable earnings?
3. How quickly can working capital unwind in a downside case?
4. Which covenant directly protects debt service coverage?
