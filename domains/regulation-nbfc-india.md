# Regulation and NBFC Context (India)

## What this domain is
The regulatory framework governing non-bank lenders, including prudential norms, provisioning, governance, and disclosures.

## Why it matters in real lending businesses
Regulation shapes growth limits, capital allocation, product design, and acceptable risk appetite.

## What I need to be fluent in
- Asset classification and provisioning logic
- Capital adequacy and leverage constraints
- Fair practices, compliance controls, and supervisory expectations

## How decisions are actually made
Risk, finance, and compliance teams calibrate product growth and portfolio mix within regulatory headroom and expected supervisory scrutiny.

## Where things break in reality
- Aggressive growth that outpaces control maturity
- Misaligned product features with compliance obligations
- Late recognition of provisioning impact on profitability

## Product/tech leverage points
- Rule-based compliance checks in credit workflows
- Provisioning impact simulation at decision stage
- Regulatory reporting automation with reconciliations

## Signals that I understand this well
- I can link a product change to regulatory implications.
- I can explain how provisioning shifts portfolio strategy.
- I can distinguish policy breach risk from credit risk.
