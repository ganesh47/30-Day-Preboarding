# Early Warning Signals

## Practical lens
Early warning is the bridge between underwriting and collections.

## Borrower stress signals
- Persistent receivable stretch and creditor pressure
- Margin compression not explained by temporary factors
- Delayed reporting, data inconsistency, audit qualifications
- Payment behavior drift before formal delinquency

## Monitoring frameworks
- Risk segmentation by borrower and facility type
- Trigger thresholds with defined escalation owners
- Monthly watchlist reviews linked to action logs

## Questions I would ask a credit team
1. Which indicators have highest lead time before delinquency?
2. Are we tracking behavior change or only accounting outcomes?
3. What percentage of alerts lead to real action?
4. How do we measure false negatives in stress detection?
