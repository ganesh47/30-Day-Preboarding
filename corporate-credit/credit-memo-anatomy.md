# Credit Memo Anatomy

## Practical lens
A strong credit memo is a decision document: approve, decline, or approve with protections.

## How credit notes are written
- Borrower overview: business model, promoters, governance
- Financial analysis: earnings quality, leverage, liquidity
- Structure recommendation: tenor, covenants, collateral, pricing
- Risks and mitigants: explicit mapping, not vague statements
- Monitoring plan: what will be tracked monthly/quarterly

## How credit committees think
- Is downside understood and survivable?
- Are assumptions realistic or sponsor-driven?
- Do protections create early intervention options?
- Is return adequate for expected and tail risk?

## Questions I would ask a credit team
1. What is the committee's explicit reason to believe repayment is robust?
2. Which assumption has the highest model sensitivity?
3. If this goes wrong, what is the first control point?
4. What is the kill-switch condition for future exposure?
