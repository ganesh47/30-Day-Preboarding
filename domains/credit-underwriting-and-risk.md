# Credit Underwriting and Risk

## What this domain is
The discipline of evaluating whether a borrower can and will repay under base and stressed conditions, and whether structure compensates for risk.

## Why it matters in real lending businesses
Bad credit decisions compound quietly. Yield looks attractive early; losses surface later through delinquency, restructuring, and write-offs.

## What I need to be fluent in
- Business model durability and cash flow quality
- Promoter behavior and governance red flags
- Leverage tolerance by sector and cycle stage
- Risk-adjusted pricing and downside coverage

## How decisions are actually made
Committee decisions blend model outputs, analyst judgment, sector context, and negotiation constraints. Final calls often hinge on downside recoverability, not upside narrative.

## Where things break in reality
- Over-reliance on historical financials
- Weak stress-testing assumptions
- Inadequate covenant package
- Slow recognition of early warning signs

## Product/tech leverage points
- Standardized underwriting checklists with exception logs
- Scenario engines for covenant headroom and DSCR under stress
- Decision audit trails for committee rationale

## Signals that I understand this well
- I can explain decline rationale in one page with quant + qualitative evidence.
- I can identify the top three loss drivers before disbursal.
- I can suggest structural fixes that change expected downside.
