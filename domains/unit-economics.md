# Unit Economics

## What this domain is
Measurement of whether each loan or segment creates value after funding cost, operating cost, credit loss, and capital usage.

## Why it matters in real lending businesses
Volume without unit profitability creates delayed balance sheet stress.

## What I need to be fluent in
- Net interest margin vs risk-adjusted margin
- Cost-to-serve by segment and channel
- Vintage performance and loss emergence curve

## How decisions are actually made
Teams make trade-offs between growth and profitability by cohort, not portfolio averages.

## Where things break in reality
- Underpriced risk in competitive segments
- Ignoring servicing cost inflation
- Optimizing approvals instead of contribution margin

## Product/tech leverage points
- Cohort-level profitability dashboards
- Decision-time expected loss and cost-to-serve estimates
- Pricing engines with guardrails by risk band

## Signals that I understand this well
- I can explain why a high-growth segment may be value-destructive.
- I can separate accounting profitability from economic profitability.
- I can propose pricing or policy moves with measurable margin impact.
