# Default and Resolution

## Practical lens
Resolution aims to maximize risk-adjusted recovery, not to win every legal argument.

## Restructuring vs default
- Restructuring works when core business viability remains and incentives can be reset.
- Default path is required when cooperation fails or value erosion accelerates.

## Insolvency process
- Process timelines materially influence net recovery.
- Seniority, collateral quality, and documentation control outcomes.
- Strategy should estimate time-to-cash, legal cost, and uncertainty.

## Negotiation dynamics
- Borrower willingness vs ability to pay must be separated.
- Concessions should be tied to verifiable milestones.
- Information asymmetry is central; data discipline is a strategic tool.

## Questions I would ask a credit team
1. What is our best realistic recovery route by time-to-cash?
2. Which concessions improve recovery probability versus delay recognition?
3. How is stakeholder alignment managed across lender groups?
4. What triggers transition from restructure to enforcement?
